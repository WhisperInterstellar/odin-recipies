Follow your rice cooker's instructions OR:

While it should be as common as a microwave or toaster, life isn't always that way. Not everyone has a rice cooker. For the sake of those who don't (which once included me), here is a rundown of how to get rice out of a pot with a tight fitting lid. This will make enough for 4 rice balls. I don't reccomend trying to make less, or to make more than 3 times as much.

You will need:

  • about 240ml (1 cp) of uncooked short grain rice
  • about 300ml (1.25 cp) of water
  • ! For "sushi" recipies, use about 5 to 10 percent less water, as there will need to be "room" in the rice for some liquid seasonings later.

  1. Measure the rice and water. Ratio 1:1.25
  2. "Wash" the rice. put the rice in a bowl and pour some running water in with it. Swirl it around to clean any dust or other residue from the grains' surfaces. Carefully pour out the cloudy water. Repeat this until the water is not particularly cloudy. It shouldn't take more than a few times to get clean enough, don't worry if it's not perfectly clear. After this, use a very clean towel (no softener or anything else you wouldn't want in your mouth!) or a large paper towel to get most of the remaining water out, possibly with some kind of strainer as well.
  3. Soak. Put your cleaned rice and the water you measured earlier in a pot together and let it soak for about 20-30 minutes
  4. Cover the pot tightly, if you haven't already. From this point on, do not remove the lid. Do not! As much steam needs to stay inside as possible!
  5. Heat the pot over medium heat until you can hear boiling.
  6. Reduce heat to low and allow the rice to simmer for 12 to 13 minutes. The precise timing can vary based on humidity, temperature, elevation, air pressure, all sorts of things. But somewhere in that time frame should be close enough. If you smell something toasty before then, stop early. If you have a clear lid and can still see water bubbling in there, keep going.
  7. Remove from heat and let it steam for 10 more minutes. Don't lift the lid until after this step. I know it's hard, but do your best to trust the rice.
  8. Finally you can lift the lid now. Use a spatula to fluff the rice and use or store it.
